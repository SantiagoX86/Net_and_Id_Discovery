## 1. Purpose

This document defines the functional requirements governing the behavior of the Enterprise Security Discovery Orchestration Framework. It establishes enforceable system-level expectations that define what the system must do, independent of implementation details.

This document is a control artifact and is not intended to track implementation progress or milestone-specific state.

---

## 2. Scope

This document applies to all functional components of the framework, including:

- Core orchestration framework
- All discovery domains
- Output and reporting capabilities
- Correlation and intelligence processing
- Execution and control interfaces
- External interface layers
- Enterprise deployment and operational readiness capabilities

All functional behavior defined herein applies uniformly across all system components.

---

## 3. System-Wide Functional Requirements

### 3.1 Deterministic Operation
- The system SHALL produce identical outputs given identical inputs.
- The system SHALL execute all operations in a deterministic and repeatable order.
- The system SHALL NOT introduce randomness or non-deterministic behavior.

### 3.2 Agentless Operation
- The system SHALL operate without deploying agents on target systems.
- The system SHALL perform all discovery remotely.

### 3.3 Non-Exploitative Behavior
- The system SHALL NOT perform exploitation, attack simulation, or vulnerability triggering.
- The system SHALL NOT alter the state of target systems.

### 3.4 No Authentication or Credential Usage
- The system SHALL NOT use, transmit, or test credentials.
- The system SHALL NOT perform authentication attempts.

### 3.5 Safe Interaction Model
- The system SHALL limit interactions to safe, low-impact methods.
- The system SHALL NOT perform state-changing operations.

---

## 4. Core Framework Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL implement a centralized orchestration framework to manage execution of discovery domains.
- The system SHALL invoke discovery modules in a deterministic sequence.
- The system SHALL isolate execution failures within individual modules.
- The system SHALL aggregate findings from all modules into a unified result set.

---

## 5. Discovery Domain Functional Requirements

### 5.1 General Requirements
- Each discovery domain SHALL operate as an independent module.
- Each discovery domain SHALL adhere to system-wide constraints defined in this document.
- Each discovery domain SHALL produce findings using the standardized `DiscoveryFinding` schema.
- Each discovery domain SHALL NOT interfere with other domains.

---

### 5.2 Network Discovery
- The system SHALL perform network-based discovery using connection-based techniques.
- The system SHALL identify host presence and port availability through safe interaction methods.
- The system SHALL NOT perform protocol negotiation or data extraction beyond connection establishment.

---

### 5.3 Identity Discovery
- The system SHALL identify exposure of identity-related services based on externally observable signals.
- The system SHALL use connection-based methods to determine service availability.
- The system SHALL NOT perform authentication, enumeration, or protocol negotiation.

---

### 5.4 Host Configuration Discovery
- The system SHALL infer host configuration posture based on previously collected findings.
- The system SHALL NOT initiate direct network communication for configuration inference.
- The system SHALL derive conclusions solely from existing structured findings.

---

### 5.5 Application / Service Discovery
- The system SHALL identify exposed application and service interfaces through externally observable indicators.
- The system SHALL operate without authentication or active interrogation of application logic.

---

### 5.6 Telemetry / Logging Exposure Discovery
- The system SHALL identify externally exposed telemetry, logging, or monitoring endpoints.
- The system SHALL NOT access or retrieve sensitive logging data.

---

## 6. Correlation and Intelligence Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL correlate findings across multiple discovery domains.
- The system SHALL generate higher-order insights based on aggregated data.
- The system SHALL maintain traceability between derived insights and source findings.

---

## 7. Output and Reporting Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL produce structured output in JSON format.
- The system SHALL provide human-readable reporting in Markdown format.
- The system SHALL include sufficient evidence to support independent validation of findings.
- The system SHALL ensure output consistency across repeated executions.

---

## 8. Execution and Control Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL accept target input through controlled execution interfaces.
- The system SHALL validate all input prior to execution.
- The system SHALL control execution flow through a centralized orchestration mechanism.
- The system SHALL NOT allow unauthorized modification of execution parameters.

---

## 9. External Interface Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL provide interfaces for consuming discovery results.
- The system SHALL support integration with external systems in a controlled manner.
- The system SHALL ensure consistent data formats across interfaces.

---

## 10. Enterprise Readiness Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL support secure deployment in enterprise environments.
- The system SHALL maintain predictable resource utilization.
- The system SHALL operate within defined safety and performance constraints.

---

## 11. Error Handling Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL capture all errors as structured events.
- The system SHALL continue execution despite individual module failures.
- The system SHALL ensure errors do not impact unrelated components.

---

## 12. Governance and Compliance Functional Requirements

- The system SHALL operate within defined security and compliance boundaries.
- The system SHALL NOT collect or expose sensitive data.
- The system SHALL maintain full auditability of execution and outputs.
