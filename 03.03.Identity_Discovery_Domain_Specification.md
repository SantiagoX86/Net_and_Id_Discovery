## 1. Purpose

The Identity Discovery Domain defines a deterministic, discovery-only domain responsible for producing direct-observation findings for externally reachable identity-related service interfaces observed through approved connect-only TCP interaction on a bounded approved identity port set.

This domain operates in an agentless, non-exploitative manner and produces normalized `DiscoveryFinding` outputs aligned with the Core Framework schema.

This domain exists to model bounded identity-service exposure without introducing authentication attempts, protocol negotiation, banner collection, handshake inspection, directory enumeration, endpoint enumeration, metadata extraction, trust discovery, or higher-order interpretation.

---

## 2. Vantage Point Assumptions

* No valid credentials are supplied.
* Discovery is executed from an external or minimally privileged network position.
* No exploitation, brute force, authentication bypass, or active interrogation techniques are used.
* Observations are limited to externally observable TCP connectivity signals on approved identity-related ports.
* No target response content is read, parsed, or interpreted.
* No upstream findings are consumed by this domain.

---

## 3. Scope

### 3.1 In Scope

* Detection of externally exposed authentication services via TCP connectivity (e.g., LDAP, Kerberos, WinRM, SMB endpoints).
* Identification of externally reachable identity-related service ports based on successful connection establishment.
* Deterministic classification of identity-related service exposure based on approved port-to-service associations following successful connect-only TCP establishment.
* Detection of externally reachable identity-related service surfaces through connectivity signals on approved identity-domain ports.

All findings must be derived from TCP connection establishment only (connect-only model). No protocol negotiation, handshake inspection, banner analysis, or metadata extraction is performed.

### 3.2 Out of Scope

* Credential spraying or brute-force attempts.
* Anonymous or authenticated LDAP directory enumeration.
* Active exploitation of identity protocols.
* Privilege escalation or token manipulation.
* Lateral movement or session hijacking.
* Any modification to frozen M1–M3 components without formal change control.

### 3.3 Relationship to Network Discovery

Network Discovery remains the authoritative generic producer of `network/open_port` findings across the approved project-scoped TCP port inventory where authorized by its governing specification.

Identity Discovery owns domain-specific direct-observation findings for approved identity-related service exposure.

Network Discovery owns the `service_hint` governed evidence field for generic Network-owned TCP port classification.

Identity Discovery owns the `identity_service_hint` governed evidence field for Identity-owned static identity-service classification.

Network Discovery may independently produce generic `network/open_port` findings for identity-related TCP ports when those ports are included in the approved project-scoped TCP port inventory.

Identity Discovery does not replace, suppress, reinterpret, or validate Network Discovery findings.

Identity Discovery MUST NOT emit generic `open_port` findings.

Identity Discovery MUST NOT emit or reuse Network-owned `service_hint`.

Network Discovery MUST NOT emit or use `identity_service_hint`.

Network Discovery MUST NOT emit `identity_service_exposed` findings.

Overlapping Network and Identity findings on the same TCP port are independently valid producer-owned observations and MUST remain distinct, unsuppressed, and traceable to their producing domains.

## 4. Technical Approach

The Identity Discovery Domain extends the existing Core Framework module contract without introducing architectural changes to frozen Milestones M1–M3. The module operates using controlled TCP-based connectivity checks to identify identity-related exposure without authentication attempts, protocol negotiation, or target state modification.

Identity protocol detection is performed strictly through TCP connection establishment (connect-only model). No protocol data is transmitted, and no handshake, negotiation, banner inspection, or metadata extraction is performed. Identity-related findings are produced as direct, deterministic discovery outputs based solely on successful connection signals on approved known ports and the governed `identity_service_hint` static port-to-classification mapping defined by this specification.

As a producing discovery domain, Identity Discovery remains discovery-only. It MUST NOT consume upstream findings for higher-order meaning assignment, perform cross-domain correlation, encode hidden inference inside evidence fields, or depend on downstream interpretation to define the meaning of its outputs.

Discovery logic reuses validated socket abstractions and error-handling patterns established in the Network Discovery Domain to preserve deterministic behavior. All interactions respect defined timeout thresholds and incorporate structured exception handling to prevent module failure propagation.

Findings are normalized through the existing finding schema and categorized according to approved identity-related categories. Confidence scoring reflects certainty based on connection success and the approved identity port-to-`identity_service_hint` association. Errors, timeouts, or unexpected conditions generate structured `identity_probe_error` events where applicable.

Implementation must be deterministic: identical environmental conditions must produce identical findings. No randomization, race conditions, or nondeterministic behavior will be introduced.

## 10. Identity Probe Strategy

The Identity Discovery Domain will employ a controlled, low-noise probing methodology designed to safely identify identity-related exposure without authentication attempts or target state modification. All probes must remain consistent with the agentless and non-exploitative design principles defined earlier in this specification.

### 10.1 Probe Design Principles

All identity probes must adhere to the following constraints:

* **Connect-only interaction:** Probes are limited strictly to TCP connection establishment. No protocol negotiation, handshake interaction, or application-layer data exchange is performed.
* **No credential transmission:** The module must never send usernames, passwords, tokens, or authentication material.
* **Low-noise operation:** Timeouts and retry logic must minimize network noise and avoid triggering defensive controls.
* **Deterministic behavior:** Identical inputs and environmental conditions must yield identical outputs.
* **Failure isolation:** Probe failures must generate structured events without interrupting orchestrator execution.

### 10.2 Initial Protocol Targets

Phase B implementation currently includes the following validated probes, with additional protocols prioritized for future expansion:

#### Validated (Implemented in Phase B)
* SMB (TCP/445) — connectivity-only exposure signal; no negotiation/authentication
* NetBIOS Session Service (TCP/139) — connectivity-only exposure signal; no session negotiation or authentication
* WinRM (TCP/5985) — connectivity-only exposure signal; no HTTP request/authentication
* Kerberos (TCP/88)
* LDAP (TCP/389)
* LDAPS (TCP/636)
* WinRM over HTTPS (TCP/5986)
* RPC Endpoint Mapper (TCP/135) — connect-only signal; no endpoint enumeration (no EPM queries)
* RDP (TCP/3389) — connect-only exposure signal; no RDP negotiation, security exchange, or authentication

#### Authorized Next (Phase B Expansion — Connectivity-Only)
* N/A

Protocol selection may expand in future milestones through formal change control.

### 10.3 Evidence Collection Strategy

A probe may generate a finding when one or more of the following conditions are observed:

* Successful TCP connection indicating service exposure on a known identity-related port.
* Consistent connection behavior aligned with expected service-port associations.

Evidence must remain strictly observational and derived from TCP connectivity only. The module must not perform protocol negotiation, handshake inspection, banner analysis, authentication attempts, or any form of metadata extraction.

Permitted evidence elements for positive findings include:

* `method`
* `port`
* `identity_service_hint`
* `timeout_s`
* Optional controlled explanatory note consistent with this specification

### 10.4 Timeout and Safety Controls

To maintain enterprise-safe behavior:

* Default socket timeout must remain low (≤ 1–2 seconds).
* No aggressive retry loops are permitted in Phase B.
* Parallel probing must be avoided until explicitly authorized in a future milestone.
* All socket operations must be wrapped in structured exception handling.

### 10.5 Finding Classification Rules

Identity-related findings generated under this strategy will map to the approved categories defined earlier in this specification. Confidence scoring will reflect the strength of deterministic service classification based on connect-only TCP success and approved service-port association.

Identity Discovery findings produced for successful connect-only TCP exposure on approved identity-domain ports MUST use:

* `domain = "identity"`
* `category = "identity_service_exposed"`
* `evidence.identity_service_hint` for Identity-owned static service classification

### 10.6 Future Expansion Guardrails

The following capabilities are explicitly deferred to later milestones and require formal review before implementation:

* Anonymous LDAP queries
* Kerberos pre-auth analysis
* SMB session metadata inspection beyond negotiation
* Any credential-based interaction

These guardrails ensure the Identity Discovery Domain remains aligned with the project’s non-exploitative mission.

### 10.7 identity_service_hint Contract Definition

#### Definition

`identity_service_hint` represents a deterministic, producer-owned classification of the identity-related service associated with a successfully established TCP connection on an approved Identity Discovery port.

It is derived strictly from:

* The destination port
* The fact that a TCP connection was successfully established
* The approved static identity port-to-classification mapping defined by this specification

No protocol interaction, negotiation, validation, response parsing, socket read, authentication, enumeration, banner inspection, handshake inspection, or metadata extraction is performed.

---

#### Constraints

`identity_service_hint` MUST:

* Be emitted only by Identity Discovery for Identity-owned findings
* Be derived solely from connect-only TCP success signals on this domain’s approved identity port set
* Be deterministic and consistent across executions
* Use a controlled, predefined vocabulary
* Represent Identity-owned service classification only
* Preserve producer-owned semantic ownership for Identity Discovery findings

`identity_service_hint` MUST NOT:

* Reuse or alias Network-owned `service_hint`
* Reuse or alias `application_service_hint`
* Reuse or alias `telemetry_logging_hint`
* Encode reachability semantics beyond the producing contract
* Imply protocol correctness, authentication capability, directory availability, domain role, trust relationship, service health, or successful application-layer interaction
* Imply authentication state, TLS state, certificate validity, endpoint availability, or response behavior
* Include inferred host roles or configurations
* Be dynamically generated or environment-dependent
* Be derived from response content, banners, handshakes, metadata, authentication results, or enumeration results

---

#### Allowed Values (Identity Domain)

The following values are the authoritative controlled vocabulary for Identity Discovery:

* `"SMB"`
* `"NetBIOS-SSN"`
* `"WinRM"`
* `"WinRM-HTTPS"`
* `"RPC"`
* `"LDAP"`
* `"LDAPS"`
* `"Kerberos"`
* `"RDP"`

No additional values are permitted without controlled specification update.

#### Cross-Domain Contract Requirements

Identity Discovery is the authoritative producing owner of `identity_service_hint`.

Network Discovery remains the authoritative producing owner of `service_hint`.

Application / Service Discovery remains the authoritative producing owner of `application_service_hint`.

Telemetry / Logging Exposure Discovery remains the authoritative producing owner of `telemetry_logging_hint`.

Producing logic MUST:

* Emit only approved `identity_service_hint` values
* Treat `identity_service_hint` as a governed evidence field
* Avoid hidden inference, reachability expansion, host-role claims, posture claims, product identification, protocol validation, authentication meaning, or downstream-layer meaning injection

Consuming domains MUST treat `identity_service_hint` as:

* Read-only
* Authoritative within the Identity Discovery producing contract
* Exact-match only
* Non-derivable outside approved consuming specifications

No domain may normalize, alias, reinterpret, extend, synonym-expand, heuristically remap, or silently convert the meaning of `identity_service_hint`.

No consuming domain may fall back from `identity_service_hint` to `service_hint` for Identity Discovery findings unless such behavior is explicitly authorized by a future controlled specification update.

---

#### Determinism Requirements

For identical inputs:

* `identity_service_hint` MUST always resolve to the same value
* Mapping between approved Identity Discovery ports and `identity_service_hint` values MUST remain fixed
* No conditional, response-derived, target-dependent, environment-based, or runtime-generated variation is allowed
